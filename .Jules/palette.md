## 2026-05-15 - Accessibility of Icon-Only Actions in Reader Contexts
**Learning:** In highly interactive reading environments (like `components/reader/professional-reader.tsx`), numerous small contextual actions (toggle subtitles, settings, highlights) are naturally designed as icon-only to save space and minimize reading distractions. While visually efficient, this creates a severe accessibility gap where screen readers hit a wall of unlabeled, vague buttons. The same pattern frequently emerges in interactive media cards (like `components/playlists/playlist-card.tsx`) for actions like Follow/Unfollow.
**Action:** When working on dense user interfaces like readers or media cards, preemptively sweep for `size="icon"` or `size="sm"` icon-only buttons. Always ensure they receive descriptive `aria-label` attributes that explicitly state the action, not just the icon name (e.g. "Toggle Subtitles" instead of "Type").
